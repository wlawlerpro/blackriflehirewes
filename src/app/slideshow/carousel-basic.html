After graduation, Wes struggled to find work. He was constantly being told that he was overqualified or did not have enough experience for positions. The company Wes founded with his family was getting off the ground but there was not enough money to support Wes and his three other family members. He would scrap everything off his resume just to be able to get a part-time job washing dishes.

In 2017, Wes would be let go from his position at Texas A&M. Wes immediately thought it would be a good idea to pick up a hard skill and chose computer programming. It was at this time that a friend of Wes’s would introduce him to the typescript coding language and the Angular programming frame work. Wes’s plan was to remain in Texas, get a new full time job, and learn this new technology. Wes failed in his plan, but did not give up learning typescript and Angular. Wes would end up having to move back home with his parents in 2018.

Today, Wes lives with his parents and loads packages for UPS. At the start of the pandemic, Wes saw that applying online for jobs was going to be a near fruitless endeavor. So he focused almost all of his time towards computer programming and graphic design.
